The Math the best part!

Following Artonico's lead, I based the strength of each card based off of a Common Score (C-Score) and an Optimal Score (O-Score).

C-Score is the premise of using a card under a common setting - easy to access boosts a player can give their team.

O-Score is the premise of using a card under an optimal setting - relatively harder to access boosts.

The raw formula for both Scores is: score = raw_stat + ((raw_stat)*(1 + SIS_percent boost) + SIS_static_boost) * (1 + center_boost)*2 + score_up_boost

Card Skill Quality

Skill quality is the average amount of points/perfect lock time/stamina healed a card's skill can contribute over the course of a song.
The calculation based off of a 125 second, 550 note long song with 65 star notes, assuming 85% Perfect Notes/15% Great Notes and Full Combo.

The closest in-game variants to this theoretical song are EX Music Start (551 notes/124 seconds), Junai Lens (550 notes/125 seconds), and Loveless World (550/126 seconds).

Why? Skills are mainly activated by seconds, perfects, and notes/combo string.

School Idol Skills

Number of School Idol Skill Slots

C- & O-Score, unidolized
2 per SR | 3 per SSR | 4 per UR
C-Score, idolized
3 per SR | 4 per SSR | 5 per UR
O-Score, idolized
4 per SR | 4 per SSR | 5 per UR

  • Why?
    • For unidolized cards, the number of slots stays constant.
    • For idolized SRs/SSRs/URs, it is more common to idolize a card with one copy and add one slot.
    • It is optimal and viable to idolize an SR twice (via ranking in Tier 1 during an event). However, it is unlikely to idolize an SSR or UR multiple times, so the number of slots for C- and O-Score are the same.
